iPhone Home Screen customization has evolved from a simple grid of apps into a highly personalized digital space. We choose custom icons, match our wallpapers, and use widgets to display our daily data at a glance.

For me, tracking daily movement is a core part of my routine. But for a long time, I struggled to find a pedometer app that looked good on my Home Screen. Most health apps are filled with bright neon colors and heavy dashboards that clash with a clean design.

That changed when I started using **Steps Widget**. It has quickly become my absolute favorite Home Screen widget on iOS. Here is a look at my favorite layouts, why they work, and how you can set them up for a clean, active day.

## 1. The Minimal Progress Ring (Small Widget)

My absolute favorite layout is the small-sized **Progress Ring** widget.

- **Why it is my favorite**: It is extremely minimal. Instead of displaying a lot of text, it shows a clean, elegant ring that fills toward your daily goal as you walk, with your current step count in the center.
- **Setup Tip**: I pick an accent color from the theme picker that matches my wallpaper, like a soft sky blue or pink, and let the widget follow light and dark mode automatically. Placing it in a corner of the Home Screen keeps the layout balanced and gives a gentle, encouraging cue every time I unlock my phone.

## 2. The Hourly Bar Chart (Small Widget)

When I am working from my desk during the week, I switch my focus from overall daily steps to hourly movement. For this, the **Hourly Chart** style is my go-to choice: a small widget showing my step total on top and an hour-by-hour bar chart below it.

- **Why it is my favorite**: The bar chart shows exactly when I walked during the day. It makes it easy to spot sedentary gaps—like sitting straight through the afternoon—and prompts me to stand up and walk.
- **Setup Tip**: Drag it on top of another small widget, like Weather or Batteries, to create a widget stack. You can swipe up or down on the stack to flip to your hourly chart during desk breaks, then flip back when you need to focus.

## 3. The Lock Screen Circle (Compact Widget)

With iOS Lock Screen widgets, you can check your progress without even unlocking your phone.

- **Why it is my favorite**: It sits right below the clock, rendering in a clean, monochromatic system style. It gives you instant, zero-click feedback on your walking progress.
- **Setup Tip**: Pair it with Apple's built-in Batteries widget to create a symmetrical, clean look below the time. Lock Screen widgets require iOS 16 or later.

## Why This is My Favorite Setup

Beyond the aesthetics, the main reason Steps Widget is my favorite utility is its **quiet, privacy-first design**.

It reads directly from the Apple Health database, meaning there are no background GPS trackers draining my battery, no online accounts to manage, and no social feeds pushing me to compete with others. It simply turns my existing step data into a beautiful, functional ornament on my Home Screen.

If you are looking to build a consistent walking habit without sacrificing your iPhone’s aesthetic, try setting up a progress ring widget and matching it to your wallpaper. It makes daily activity feel simple, visual, and completely natural.
