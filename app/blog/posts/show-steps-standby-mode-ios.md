iOS StandBy mode transforms your iPhone into a smart glanceable display when it is charging on its side. It is perfect for displaying clocks, weather forecasts, and calendar events while sitting at your desk or sleeping on your nightstand.

However, if you want to use StandBy mode to keep an eye on your daily walking progress, you might run into a frustrating limitation: **Apple's built-in Health and Fitness apps do not offer a step counter widget for the StandBy interface.**

Fortunately, you can easily show your steps in iOS StandBy mode using a third-party app. Here is a step-by-step guide to setting up a custom, aesthetic pedometer on your locked screen.

## Why Native Apple Apps Fall Short in StandBy Mode

While Apple Health stores all of your motion data, its widgets only support showing activity rings (move, exercise, stand) or overall weekly averages. If you simply want to see your raw step count (e.g., "4,250 steps") at a glance, Apple's native widgets cannot help you on the StandBy screen.

To bridge this gap, you need a third-party widget app like **StepsWidget** that is specifically designed to read your step data from Apple Health and format it as a `systemSmall` widget, which is the exact size required by the StandBy interface.

## How to Set Up a Steps Widget in StandBy Mode

Before you begin, make sure your iPhone is running iOS 17 or later, as StandBy mode is not available on older operating system versions.

### Step 1: Download StepsWidget & Enable Permissions

If you haven't already, install StepsWidget on your iPhone.

- Open the app.
- When prompted, grant permission for the app to access your step count from Apple Health. (Your data stays completely private and local on your device).

### Step 2: Activate StandBy Mode

Ensure that StandBy is enabled in your system settings:

- Open your iPhone **Settings**.
- Tap **StandBy** and toggle it on.
- Connect your iPhone to a charger (either a MagSafe stand, wireless charger, or standard lightning/USB-C cable).
- Lock your iPhone and place it in **landscape (horizontal) orientation**.
- After a second, the StandBy screen will illuminate automatically.

### Step 3: Add StepsWidget to Your StandBy Display

- Once StandBy mode is active, **long-press (tap and hold)** on either the left or right side of the screen.
- Authenticate with Face ID or enter your passcode if prompted.
- Tap the **"+" (plus) button** in the top-left corner of the editing screen.
- Scroll down the list of apps or use the search bar to find **StepsWidget**.
- Swipe to choose your preferred small widget style (such as the minimalist number or the clean progress ring).
- Tap **Add Widget**.
- Tap **Done** in the top-right corner to save your layout.

## Keeping Steps Glanceable on Your Desk

Having your step counter visible in StandBy mode is especially helpful for office and remote workers.

Instead of picking up your phone, unlocking it, and navigating to a health app—which often leads to getting distracted by social media or email—you can see your movement progress with a simple glance to the side.

Because StepsWidget offers curated, color-matched themes (such as Sky Blue, Accent Pink, or automatic Dark Mode matching), your step counter can blend seamlessly with your desk decor and wallpaper colors, even when your phone is locked and charging.
