The Apple Watch is an incredible companion for daily fitness. However, because Apple's design philosophy focuses on active energy (calories) and active minutes, your exact step count can sometimes feel secondary.

Understanding how to access, track, and calibrate your daily step data is key to building a consistent movement habit. 

This guide outlines how to view your Apple Watch steps, customize coaching notifications, and put your progress on the screens you check most.

## Using the Activity app to check your daily and weekly activity progress

The built-in Activity app on your Apple Watch tracks your steps automatically in the background.

- Press the Digital Crown on your Apple Watch to view your app grid, then tap the Activity app icon (the three colored rings).

- Scroll down using the Digital Crown or swipe up on the screen to view details past your Move, Exercise, and Stand rings.

- You will find your active step count, total daily walking distance, and flights of stairs climbed near the bottom of the scroll screen.

- To check weekly progress, scroll to the bottom of the main Activity screen and tap Weekly Summary to view your averages and ring completions for the current week.

## How to review your activity history and achievements

To review your step history, trends, and awards over time, use the Fitness and Health apps on your iPhone.

- **Fitness History**: Open the Fitness app on your iPhone and select the Summary tab. Tap the Activity rings panel at the top to access a monthly calendar view. Tap any individual day on the calendar to view your step counts, detailed charts, and workouts for that day.

- **Activity Trends**: Below the rings on the Summary tab, you will find the Trends section. This compares your last 90 days of activity to your 365-day baseline to show whether you are trending up or down. Note that Trends tracks metrics like walking distance and walking pace rather than raw steps, so for a step-specific trend, use the Steps chart in the Health app instead.

- **Earning Achievements**: Scroll to the bottom of the Summary screen to browse your Awards gallery. Earning badges for monthly goals, streaks, or limited-edition holidays is a fun way to stay motivated.

- **Health Database**: For raw data archives, open the Health app, tap the Browse tab, select Activity, and tap Steps. Here you can view daily, weekly, monthly, and yearly charts, or show a chronological database of every recorded day.

## Customizing your Apple Watch step tracking: reminders and coaching controls

To prevent notification fatigue, you can customize how and when your watch alerts you about your progress.

- Open the Watch app on your iPhone and select the My Watch tab.

- Tap Activity from the settings list.

- Toggle Stand Reminders on or off depending on whether you want an alert at 50 minutes past the hour.

- Adjust Daily Coaching alerts to receive personalized tips based on your progress towards your goals.

- Customize Goal Completions to control whether your watch vibrates when you hit your daily targets.

## Third-party apps that show steps on your Apple Watch face

Apple Watch does not offer a native "Steps" complication for its watch faces, only rings. If you want to see your steps on your watch face, you can use third-party apps.

- Apps like Steps Widget, Pedometer++, or StepsApp read your Apple Health step data and provide custom complications.

- To add a complication, long-press your Apple Watch face, tap Edit, swipe to Complications, select a slot, and choose your step app.

- This allows you to monitor your raw step number immediately when you raise your wrist.

## How to improve your Apple Watch step count accuracy

If you think your watch is undercounting or overcounting your steps, you can calibrate its motion sensors.

- **Snug Wrist Fit**: Ensure your Apple Watch is worn snugly on the top of your wrist. A loose watch face can fail to register body movement accurately.

- **Sensor Calibration**: Take your iPhone and Apple Watch to a flat, open outdoor area with good GPS reception. Open the Workouts app on your watch, select Outdoor Walk, and walk at your normal pace for 20 minutes to calibrate your accelerometer.

- **Update Health Metrics**: Open the Health app on your iPhone, tap your profile picture, select Health Details, and verify your Height, Weight, Age, and Sex are correct, as iOS uses these metrics to estimate your stride length.

## Key Takeaways

Keep these core tips in mind for managing your daily steps:

- Scroll down in the Apple Watch Activity app to find daily steps and weekly summaries.

- Review long-term trends and calendar history in the iPhone Fitness and Health apps.

- Calibrate your watch sensors with a 20-minute outdoor walk for maximum step count accuracy.
